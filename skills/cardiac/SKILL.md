# Cardiac Red Flags
Evaluate urgency for cardiac-related symptoms based on the following criteria:
- EMERGENCY: Chest pain radiating to the left arm, jaw, or back; severe chest pressure/tightness; chest pain accompanied by shortness of breath, diaphoresis (sweating), or nausea/vomiting.
- URGENT: Mild to moderate chest discomfort with a history of heart issues; new but non-severe palpitations without dizziness.
- NORMAL: Brief, sharp chest pain that worsens with breathing, coughing, or twisting (likely musculoskeletal); brief twinges of pain.
