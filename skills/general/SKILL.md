# General Red Flags
Evaluate urgency for general symptoms based on the following criteria:
- EMERGENCY: Sudden severe headache ("worst of life"); sudden weakness, drooping, or numbness on one side; seizures; high fever with stiff neck.
- URGENT: High fever persisting for days; severe abdominal pain; persistent vomiting or diarrhea leading to dehydration.
- NORMAL: Mild headache, mild fatigue, low-grade fever, minor stomach bug, feeling generally unwell without extreme pain.
