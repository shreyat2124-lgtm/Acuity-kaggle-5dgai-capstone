# Respiratory Red Flags
Evaluate urgency for respiratory-related symptoms based on the following criteria:
- EMERGENCY: Severe shortness of breath at rest; inability to speak in full sentences; bluish lips or face; severe wheezing with sudden onset.
- URGENT: Moderate difficulty breathing; worsening asthma symptoms not fully responding to inhaler; persistent cough with thick green/yellow phlegm and high fever.
- NORMAL: Mild cough, runny nose, or mild wheezing associated with a known cold or mild allergies; clears throat easily; breathing comfortably.
