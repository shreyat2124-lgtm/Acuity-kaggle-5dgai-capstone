# Trauma Red Flags
Evaluate urgency for trauma and injury-related symptoms based on the following criteria:
- EMERGENCY: Unconsciousness or altered mental state after head injury; uncontrolled arterial bleeding; visible bone protruding from skin (open fracture); severe burns on face/hands.
- URGENT: Suspected closed fractures (swelling, inability to bear weight, deformity); deep cuts requiring stitches but bleeding is controlled; minor head injury with persistent headache but no loss of consciousness.
- NORMAL: Minor scrapes, bruises, or shallow cuts; mild sprains that can bear some weight; bleeding that stops with pressure.
